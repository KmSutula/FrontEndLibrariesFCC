How to use

Headings

  • Use # in front of your text, with a space after
  • 1-6 # for the heading type (h1-h6)
  • Example:
    # your text

Paragraphs

  • type text normally, leave a line of space between different paragraphs
  • Example:
    Here's paragraph one

    Here's paragraph two

Bold

  • add two asterisks (**) before and after the word or phrase
  • Example:
    I just love **bold text**

Italic

  • add one asterisk (*) or underscore(_) before and after the word or phrase
  • Example:
    I just love *italicized text*
    OR
    I just love _italicized text_

Blockquote

  • add a > in front of a paragraph
  • if your quote is multiple lines, use a > at the beginning of every line
  • Example:
    > I have a dream. -MLK

Lists

    Unordered Lists

  • add dashes (-), asterisks (*), or plus signs (+) in front of line items
  • indent one or more items to create a nested list
  • Example:
    - First Item
    OR
    * First Item
    OR
    + First Item

    • Ordered Lists

  • add line items with numbers followed by periods (1.) The numbers donâ€™t have to be in numerical order, but the list should start with the number one.
  • indent one or more items to create a nested list
  • Example:
    1. First Item

Links

  • enclose the link text in brackets and then follow it immediately with the URL in parentheses
  • Example:
    [Go to Twitter](https://www.twitter.com)

Images

  • add an exclamation mark (!), followed by alt text in brackets, and the path or URL to the image asset in parentheses
  • You can optionally add a title in quotation marks after the path or URL.
  • Example:
    ![My dog is the cutest](/assets/images/cutestdogever.jpg "German Shepherd")
